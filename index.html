Ancient Egypt

3100 BC – 525 BC

Ancient Egypt did not have a single national flag. Instead, symbols were used to represent religion, royal power, and divine authority.

  • Symbols
  • Religion
  • Pharaohs

Achaemenid Empire

525 BC – 330 BC

Egypt in this period was a part of the Achaemenid Empire (Iran now). The most famous leaders are Cyrus the Great, Xerxes I, and Cambyses II.

  • Empire
  • Persian Rule
  • Dynasty

Roman Empire

30 BC – 395 AD

Egypt became a province of the Roman Empire. Roman rule influenced administration, economy, and military structure in Egypt.

  • Empire
  • Province
  • Roman Rule

Byzantine Empire

395 AD – 641 AD

Egypt was ruled by the Byzantine Empire after the fall of the Roman Empire. The most famous leaders of Byzantine Empire are Constantine I the Great, Justinian I, and Basil II.

  • Empire
  • Eastern Roman
  • Christian Rule

Rashidun Caliphate

641 AD – 661 AD

The Rashidun Caliphate was the first Muslim caliphate in Egypt. It is distinguished by their generous and kind leaders. The most famous leaders are Omar ibn Al-Khattab, Othman ibn Affan, and Ali ibn Abi Taleb.

  • Caliphate
  • Islamic
  • First Caliphate

Ummayad Caliphate

661 AD – 750 AD

After Muawiya ibn Abi Sufyan changed the name of the Rashidun Caliphate to the Ummayad Caliphate, Egypt became a part of it. The most famous leaders are Muawiya ibn Abi Sufyan, Yazid ibn Muawiya, and Marwan ibn Al-Hakam.

  • Caliphate
  • Islamic
  • Dynasty

Abbasid Caliphate

750 AD – 1258 AD

After the fall of the Ummayad Caliphate, Egypt became part of the Abbasid Caliphate, though Egypt wasn't ruled by them throughout this entire period. During some years, Egypt was ruled by other caliphates like the Fatimid and the Ayyubid. Famous leaders include Harun al-Rashid (Abbasid), Saladin (Ayyubid), and Al-Mu'tasim (Fatimid).

  • Caliphate
  • Islamic
  • Multiple Rulers

Mamluk Sultanate

1250 AD – 1517 AD

After the fall of the Abbasid Caliphate (due to the Mongols) and other caliphates like the Fatimid and Ayyubid, the Mamluk Sultanate was established in Egypt and annexed Al-Sham (Syria, Lebanon, and Palestine) and Al-Hijaz. The most famous leaders are Izz al-Din Aibek, Al-Zahir Baibars, and Muhammad ibn Qalawun.

  • Sultanate
  • Islamic
  • Military

Ottoman Empire

1517 AD – 1882 AD

After the Battle of al-Raydaniyya, the Mamluk Sultanate collapsed and the Ottoman Empire occupied Al-Sham, Al-Hijaz, and Egypt. The most famous Ottoman leaders are Selim I, Murad III, and Mustafa I. The most famous Egyptian governors are Muhammad Ali Pasha, Khedive Ismail, and Ali Bey al-Kabir.

  • Empire
  • Ottoman Rule
  • Ottoman Dynasty

Khedivate & Sultanate of Egypt

1882 AD – 1922 AD

After the United Kingdom occupied Egypt, it became the Khedivate (1882-1914) and then the Sultanate (1914-1922) with the same flag. The most famous leaders in this period are Fouad I (Sultan), Abbas Helmy II (Khedive), and Khedive Tawfiq.

  • Khedivate
  • Sultanate
  • British Occupation

Kingdom of Egypt

1922 AD – 1952 AD

After the 28th February Statement in 1922, Fouad I announced that he became the King and Egypt became a Kingdom. The most famous kings are Fouad I, Farouk I, and Ahmed Fouad II.

  • Kingdom
  • Monarchy
  • Independence

Arab Republic of Egypt

1952 AD – 1958 AD

After the 1952 Revolution, Egypt became a republic for the first time. During this period, there were only two presidents: Mohamed Naguib and Gamal Abdel Nasser.

  • Republic
  • Revolution
  • Egyptian Rule

United Arab Republic

1958 AD – 1972 AD

The United Arab Republic was simply a unity between Egypt and Syria. The most famous presidents during this period were Gamal Abdel Nasser and Anwar Sadat.

  • Union
  • Egypt-Syria
  • Arab Unity

Arab Republic of Egypt

1972 AD – Present

The Arab Republic of Egypt has continued until the present day. The most famous presidents in this era are Anwar Sadat, Hosni Mubarak, and Abdel Fattah el-Sisi.

  • Republic
  • Modern Egypt
  • Ongoing
